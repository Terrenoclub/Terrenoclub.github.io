3. What Information We Collect

3.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Membership applications: Name, address, phone number, email, date of birth, emergency contact information
  • Booking information: Court reservations, guest registrations
  • Payment information: Billing address, payment method details (processed securely through our payment providers)
  • Communication: Messages sent through our contact forms, emails, or WhatsApp
  • Emergency information: Medical conditions or emergency contacts (when voluntarily provided)

3.2 Information We Collect Automatically

  • Website usage: IP address, browser type, pages visited, time spent on site, referring websites
  • Cookies: Small files stored on your device to improve website functionality (see Cookie Policy below)
  • Security footage: CCTV recordings in common areas for safety and security purposes
  • Access logs: Entry/exit times when using member key cards or check-in systems

3.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Payment processors: Transaction confirmations and payment status
  • Emergency services: If contacted during an incident at our facilities

4. How We Use Your Information

4.1 Membership Management

  • Processing membership applications and renewals
  • Managing access to club facilities
  • Billing and payment processing
  • Communicating about your membership status

4.2 Service Provision

  • Court booking and scheduling
  • Guest registration and access
  • Providing club services and amenities
  • Equipment rental tracking

4.3 Communication

  • Sending membership confirmations and updates
  • Club news, events, and promotional offers (with your consent)
  • Responding to inquiries and customer service requests
  • Emergency notifications affecting club operations

4.4 Safety and Security

  • Monitoring club premises for safety and security
  • Investigating incidents or rule violations
  • Ensuring compliance with club rules and policies

4.5 Legal Compliance

  • Complying with legal obligations
  • Protecting our legal rights and interests
  • Preventing fraud or illegal activities

5. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data based on:

  • Contract: To provide membership services and fulfill our contractual obligations
  • Legitimate interests: For security, safety, and operational purposes
  • Consent: For marketing communications and non-essential services (you can withdraw consent at any time)
  • Legal obligation: To comply with applicable laws and regulations

6. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your data with:

6.1 Service Providers

  • Payment processors: For secure payment processing
  • IT service providers: For website hosting and maintenance
  • Communication services: Email and messaging platforms
  • Legal and professional advisors: For legal compliance and business advice

6.2 Legal Requirements

  • Authorities: When required by law, court order, or to protect rights and safety
  • Emergency services: In case of medical emergencies or safety incidents

6.3 Business Transfers

In the event of merger, acquisition, or sale of business assets (with appropriate data protection measures).

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to:

  • Active membership: Duration of membership plus 3 years for legal/tax purposes
  • Former members: 3 years after membership termination for legal and accounting requirements
  • Website visitors: 2 years for analytics and security purposes
  • CCTV footage: 30 days unless needed for incident investigation (up to 1 year)
  • Financial records: 7 years as required by Spanish tax law

8. International Data Transfers

Your data is primarily processed within the European Union. If we transfer data outside the EU, we ensure adequate protection through:

  • Adequacy decisions: Transfers to countries with adequate data protection
  • Standard contractual clauses: EU-approved contracts for data protection
  • Other appropriate safeguards: As approved by data protection authorities

9. Your Rights Under GDPR

You have the right to:

9.1 Access

  • Request a copy of your personal data we hold
  • Obtain information about how we process your data

9.2 Rectification

Correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

9.3 Erasure (“Right to be Forgotten”)

Request deletion of your personal data (subject to legal retention requirements).

9.4 Restriction of Processing

Limit how we use your personal data in certain circumstances.

9.5 Data Portability

  • Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Transfer your data to another service provider

Withdraw consent for processing at any time (where consent is the legal basis).

To exercise these rights, contact us at: info@terrenoclub.es.

10. Cookies and Website Technology

10.1 What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us improve your website experience.

10.2 Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential cookies: Necessary for website functionality (cannot be disabled)
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website
  • Marketing cookies: Used to deliver relevant advertisements (with your consent)

10.3 Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling certain cookies may affect website functionality.

11. Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption: Secure data transmission and storage
  • Access controls: Limited access to authorized personnel only
  • Regular security assessments: Ongoing monitoring and improvement
  • Staff training: Regular data protection training for employees
  • Incident response: Procedures for handling data breaches
